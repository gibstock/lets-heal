How to Create Your Own Altar

The Many Faced Altar

An altar has many different forms and has shown up across the world as a spiritual hearth for families and individuals.

The purpose of having a personal altar is to have a place that can provide solitude and the essential elements needed to focus energy into a specific intention. The ritual act of adorning this sacred space with symbolic decorations and lighting a candle while meditating on life choices can bring a lot of power into your life.

Let's Begin

Now it is time to set up and there are so many symbols, Gods/Goddesses, herbs, colors, and candles to choose from. Use your imagination and make sure you create a space that is inspirational to YOU!

First you need a table or some type of surface that will go undisturbed. There will be candles and incantations lit on fire so make sure there is a safe distance from flammable things. It is also a good idea to have some storage for candles, herbs, stones, and other tools for rituals.

Things to put on the altar include bells, candle holders, incense burners, flower vases, statues, and sacred objects. It is good to have saucers and bowls along with a little water for putting out matches after lighting the candles and incense.

The Five Elements

Find things to represent the Five elements Air, Earth, Fire, Water, and Spirit.

Air

Air symbolizing wind, communication, and thought can be represented by bells, incense smoke, fairies, turquoise, and poetry.

Earth

Earth symbolizing body, planet, matter, and mother represented by plants, money, pine cones, elves, and Jade.

Fire

Fire symbolizes energy, movement, passion, and power represented by candles, cauldrons, lights, and ruby.

Water

Water symbolizes emotion, communication, friendship, and flow represented by chalices, wine, flowers in water, ocean stones, shells, and opal.

Spirit

Finally Spirit symbolizes psyche, soul, cosmos, and intention and can be represented by statues, paintings, chants, crystal balls, mirrors, and amethyst.

Arrangement

These chosen items can be arranged however you like but a Pentagram is a popular symbol. But there are many ways to set up an altar and it should be suited to your own lifestyles and beliefs. We will have many examples to come.

How do I use the Altar?

Now that your altar is arranged and you have an intention in mind or an invocation you would like to practice all you need to do is relax and enjoy the fruits of your labor.

  • Light the candles and incense so the aromas are swirling around you.
  • Do some deep breathing exercises and concentrate on becoming relaxed and focusing your energy through breathing.
  • Take in the beautiful arrangement that you have lovingly assembled.
  • Perhaps ring your bell to increase the vibrations.
  • Then choose a mantra or invocation to say aloud or in your head.
  • Close your eyes and cast a circle of protection around your ritual space.
  • You can repeat this with alternate moments of peaceful silence.
  • Visualize what you want out of this ritual and what your intentions are for your peace of mind.
  • Or visualize a specific God/Goddess that you would like to invoke the powers of into your life.
  • Once you feel you have communed with the spirits, the elements, or maybe yourself enough than make sure you are thankful to spirits, the elements, and especially yourself for putting in this time.
  • Now feel yourself become grounded again to the earth as the power you have drawn up settles down.
  • Open the sacred circle with your minds eye and close it permanently around your altar.
  • Say aloud:
    • For the good of all
    • And with harm to none
    • The spell is sealed
    • And the magic is done
  • Or you can just have this peaceful moment in silence as long as you are present and walk away feeling empowered and euphoric.
